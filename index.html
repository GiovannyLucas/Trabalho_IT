What Are Computers?

Computers are machines created since before World War II with the intention of performing a function. Functions of calculation, or even decoding, as was the case in the second war, where a computer and its creator gave the victory to his homeland for this reason. The interaction between software (logical part) and hardware (physical part) causes faster tasks to be performed and brings the user a sense of reality in certain activities. Previously, they were gigantic machines, occupied large rooms and were difficult to access. Today, they fit in the palm of the hand and do even more incredible things beyond encryption. They serve both for domestic and school use, military, etc. Recent research shows that there has been a considerable increase in the number of people who own a computer at home. Few know about the evolution process of computers, until the modern state-of-the-art technology models of today. Following the chronological evolution of computers:
- 1946: The creation of the world's first large-scale electronic digital computer, ENIAC (Electrical Numerical Integrator and Calculator), is announced.
- 1951 to 1959: arise the first generation computers. These were able to calculate at a milliseconds speed, in addition to being programmed in machine language.
- 1959 to 1965: arise the computers of second generation, with capacity of calculating with a speed of microseconds, being programmed in assembly language.
- 1965 to 1975: the computers of the third generation are born. These computers now have a number of miniaturized components mounted on a single CHIP, being able to compute in nanoseconds with a high-level, procedure-oriented programming language.
- 1975 to 1981: Fourth generation computers are created. Following the trend of the third generation of miniaturization of its components and the improvement of its Integrated Circuits (IC). The languages ​​used in this generation were of the highest level, oriented to a problem.
- 1990: since that decade, software of better quality and with the capacity to process information more quickly has been launched.
- 2000: after the turn of the millennium computers continued to follow the trend of miniaturizing their components and thus making computers more malleable and practical in daily tasks. In addition, there is a massive investment in your design.
Things are changing fast as the world progresses. In this modern world, people are using the machines to do their job. The computer is the most important and advanced machine in the world, because it is fast to use efficient and a multitasking machine.
The sky is the limit.
